How simple interest is calculated

interest = principal × rate ÷ 100 × time (years)
total = principal + interest

Unlike compound interest, simple interest is always calculated on the original principal only — it never earns interest on interest. That makes it easy to verify by hand and common for short-term loans, car loans and some bonds.

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal for the whole term. Compound interest is recalculated periodically on principal plus previously earned interest, so it grows faster over time. Use our Compound Interest Calculator for that case.

Months are divided by 12 and days are divided by 365 to get a time value in years before applying the formula. For example, 6 months is treated as 0.5 years.
