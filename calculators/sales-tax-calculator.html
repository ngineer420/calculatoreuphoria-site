    How sales tax is calculated

    total = price × (1 + rate ÷ 100)
    price = total ÷ (1 + rate ÷ 100)

    Sales tax rates vary by country, state, county and even city, and some categories of goods are taxed differently or exempt entirely. Enter your local combined rate — this calculator applies a single flat rate on top of (or backed out of) the price you give it.

    Check your state or region's department of revenue website, or look at the tax line on a recent receipt — it usually shows the combined rate already applied. Combined rates often stack a state rate with county and city rates.

    Because the tax rate is a percentage of the pre-tax price, not the total. A $107.25 total at 7.25% tax isn't $107.25 minus 7.25% — it's $107.25 divided by 1.0725, which correctly gives back $100.00.

    For general estimates only — always confirm exact rates and taxable items with your local tax authority. See our Terms of Use.
