    How aspect ratio scaling works

    new height = new width × (original height ÷ original width)
    new width = new height × (original width ÷ original height)

    An aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between width and height, usually written as W:H (like 16:9 or 4:3). To resize without distorting an image or video, the new width and height must keep that same ratio — this calculator solves for whichever dimension you didn't type.

    Common aspect ratios

    RatioTypical use
    16:9HD/4K video, widescreen monitors
    4:3Older TVs and monitors
    1:1Square social media posts
    9:16Vertical video, phone screens
    3:2DSLR photography

    Both the width and height are divided by their greatest common divisor (GCD). For 1920×1080, the GCD is 120, so 1920 ÷ 120 = 16 and 1080 ÷ 120 = 9, giving 16:9.

    That happens when width and height are resized independently without preserving the original ratio. Use the calculated dimension from this tool instead of guessing, and most image and video editors have a "lock aspect ratio" or "constrain proportions" option that does this automatically.
