    How date math works

    Days between two dates counts every full calendar day from the start date up to the end date. Adding or subtracting days moves forward or backward on the calendar, correctly rolling over month and year boundaries — including leap years.

    The count reflects the number of nights or full days that pass between the two dates — for example, January 1 to January 3 is 2 days apart. If you need an inclusive count (counting both the start and end day), add 1 to the result.
