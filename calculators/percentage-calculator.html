    How percentage formulas work

    Percentage of a number

    result = (percentage ÷ 100) × number

    To find 20% of 50: (20 ÷ 100) × 50 = 10.

    What percent is X of Y

    percent = (X ÷ Y) × 100

    To find what percent 15 is of 60: (15 ÷ 60) × 100 = 25%.

    Percent change

    change % = ((new − old) ÷ old) × 100

    Going from 50 to 65 is a change of ((65 − 50) ÷ 50) × 100 = 30% increase. A negative result means a decrease.

    Multiply the number by the percentage, then divide by 100. For example, 20% of 50 is (20/100) × 50 = 10.

    Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example, 15 out of 60 is (15/60) × 100 = 25%.

    Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, then multiply by 100. A positive result is an increase; a negative result is a decrease.
