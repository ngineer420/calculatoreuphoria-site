    How sleep cycle timing works

    A full sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and moves through light sleep, deep sleep and REM sleep. Waking up at the end of a cycle — rather than in the middle of deep sleep — tends to leave you feeling more rested, even with fewer total hours. This calculator counts in whole 90-minute cycles and adds roughly 15 minutes for the average time it takes to fall asleep.

    bedtime = wake time − 15 min − (cycles × 90 min)
    wake time = bedtime + 15 min + (cycles × 90 min)

    90 minutes is the commonly cited average length of one full sleep cycle in adults. Individual cycle length varies from about 70 to 120 minutes, so treat the suggested times as a helpful starting point rather than an exact science.

    Most adults do well with 5 to 6 full cycles (7.5 to 9 hours) per night. Fewer than 4 cycles generally isn't enough sleep for most people, even if it lines up with a cycle boundary.
